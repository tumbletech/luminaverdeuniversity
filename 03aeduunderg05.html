Welcome to the College of Education

The College of Education at Lumina Verde University is dedicated to nurturing the next generation of educators who will shape the future of Mali and beyond. Established to address the nation's need for well-trained teachers and education leaders, the college combines a strong foundation in pedadogy with a deep understanding of the socio-cultural and linguistic diversity of Mali.


History and Establishment

The College of Education was founded in 1998 as part of LVU's commitment to support national development. It began as a small teacher training program and has since expanded into a comprehensive college offering undergraduate and graduate programs in education. Over the years, the college has partnered with international organizations and government agencies to improve education access, teacher quality, and curriculum development across Mali.

Achievements
  • National Recognition: Regularly ranked among the top education colleges in Mali for teacher training and educational research.
  • Teacher Placement: Over 90% of graduates are employed in schools, universities, or NGOs within a year of graduation.
  • Community Impact: Developed literacy programs and teaching materials for underserved regions in Mali.
  • Innovations: Pioneered the use of technology in teacher training through online resources and blended learning approaches.

Mission

To develop highly skilled, ethical, and innovative educators who inspire and lead in diverse educational settings.

Vision

To be a leader in education by fostering excellence in teaching, learning, and research while promoting inclusivity and sustainability.

Values
  1. Empowerment: Equipping educators to transform lives and communities.
  2. Innovation: Embracing modern teaching methods and tools.
  3. Inclusivity: Valuing diversity in education for all learners.
Curriculum Design

Core Focus Areas:

  1. Teacher Preparation: Comprehensive training in pedadogy, child psychology, and inclusive education.
  2. Community Engagement: Field experiences in rural and urban schools to address Mali's diverse educational needs.
  3. Cultural Relevance: Emphasis on integrating Mali's heritage, traditions, and languages into teaching.
