Welcome to the College of Engineering

The College of Engineering at Lumina Verde University is a dynamic and forward-thinking institution committed to shaping the engineers of tomorrow. Established in 1998, the college has steadily grown from a single department to a multidisciplinary hub of innovation, research, and excellence.


History and Growth

The College of Engineering began as a modest program offering a degree in Civil Engineering to address the growing need for infrastructure development in Mali. In 2005, Electrical Engineering and Mechanical Engineering programs were added in the college, responding to the country's burgeoning energy and industrial sectors.

Despite initial challenges such as limited access to state-of-the-art facilities and shortage of qualified faculty, the college persevered. Through strategic partnerships with international institutions and industries, it acquired cutting-edge equipment and attracted top-notch faculty from around the globe.


Achievements

The College of Engineering is proud to have produced over 3,500 graduates who are driving change in critical sectors such as construction, renewable energy, manufacturing, and agriculture. It is recognized as a leader in Mali for fostering innovation, with students regularly winning national and international competitions in sustainable engineering design.

Among its notable achievements are:

  • The development of solar-powered irrigation systems for rural farming communities, significantly boosting agricultural productivity.
  • The creation of a low-cost housing prototype that utilizes local materials to address Mali's housing shortage.
  • Collaboration with international organizations on research projects aimed at improving infrastructure resilience in sub-Saharan Africa.
Vision for the Future

The College of Engineering envisions itself as a driving force for technological and sustainable development in Mali and beyond. It continues to expand its curriculum to include emerging fields such as renewable energy engineering, biomedical engineering, and environmental systems engineering.

Through rigorous academics, hands-on training, and community engagement, the college aims to produce graduates who are not only technically proficient but also socially responsible leaders prepared to tackle global challenges.
